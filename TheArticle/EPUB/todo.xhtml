Phonetic Markup

I need to finish the phonetic markup. This will require listening to as many ePub viewers as I can find that have TTS support so I can identify what is not correctly pronounced.

I am currently in the process of setting up a database of words that need phonetic markup to make it easier to identify usage in the future and create a PLS lexicon that includes them automatically. I just found out that for the past 35 years, I have been pronouncing coerulea wrong. How embarassing, how embarassing. In that particular case, think ‘Caeser’ like the salad dressing and not ‘Kaiser’ like the German Emporer.

This is high priority even though very few ePub readers currently implement phonetic markup.

Cover Art

The cover art is technically not a Tulare Kangaroo Rat, but it is in the public domain. I am partial to both the style and to work that is in the public domain, even though the propertions of the head and legs and feet are wrong.

The original plate is from 1860 and is apparently from a report on an expedition to the Pacific Northwest written by a George Suckley and James G. Cooper. The plate is identified as Dipodomys agilis but the species currently recognized by that name is restricted to Baja California and Southern California. The California Kangaroo Rat (Dipodomys californicus) ranges into Oregon and is I suspect what the plate represents. That species was not described until 1890, 30 years after the plate was created.

If at all possible, I would like to identify the actual illustrator for the plate. That is medium priority, it is not essential to identify the artist for legal use, but I do like content creators to be properly credited.

Also, if funds allow, it would be nice to have the illustration recreated to reflect correct proportions and the diagnostic characteristics of the Tulare Kangaroo Rat. This is low ‘would be nice’ priority. I also want a rainbow unicorn.

Finally, whether or not the illustration is updated, I really like the aged paper look, especially for a paper that is almost 80 years old. I would like an artist to extend that aged paper look so that it is the backdrop for the entire cover. Again, low ‘would be nice’ priority.

Photographic Plates

The two photographic plates are low quality low resolution scans.

Especially for Plate 1 but also for Plate 2, I would like to find an original print copy of the journal in good condition so I can photograph the plates using a DSLR mounted on a copy stand. This would result in much higher quality reproductions that could be restored with photoshop and then included in the ePub at a decent resolution, such as 768 pixels in width. I consider this to be a medium priority, it would greatly enhance the quality of the ePub but is not essential.

While I do not feel comfortable replacing the photographs in article with modern photographs, with luck and several trips to the San Joaquin Valley, I (or others) may be able to obtain modern equivalent photographs that demonstrate the same image for Plate 1a, Plate 1b, Plate 1c, and Plate 1d (sans shovel) as supplemental content. I do not think it is realistic to try to find equivalents for the other photographs from Plate 1.

Photographs from Plate 2 would require the assistance of a lab breeding the species to replicate, and I do not even know where to find such a lab. It might be possible to obtain similar photographs from a zoo participating in breeding the endangered Morro Bay Kangaroo Rat (Dipodomys heermanni morroensis) although the growth stages may differ.

Replication of photographs from the plates I consider to be low ‘if opportunity presents itself’ priority. Note that Plate 1f would require the destruction of a burrow to reproduce, which would not be a responsible action within the scope of citizen science.
